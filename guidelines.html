Conference Guidelines

3rd IEEE International Conference on Trends in Quantum Computing & Emerging Business Technologies (TQCEBT 2026)

24–26 March 2026 | CHRIST University, Pune Lavasa Campus

Paper Submission Guidelines

  • Submit original, unpublished work only and comply with IEEE’s anti-plagiarism policy.
  • Manuscripts must follow the IEEE conference template (A4, two-column) and be ≤ 6 pages including figures and references.
  • Use the official IEEE templates: IEEE Conference Manuscript Templates.
  • Submit via CMT: https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/TQCEBT2026.
  • You may edit until the deadline; late submissions are not accepted.

Important Dates

  • Abstract Submission: 23 October 2025
  • Full Paper Submission: 23 December 2025
  • Acceptance Notification: 10 January 2026
  • Final Paper Submission: 11 February 2026
  • Conference: 24–26 March 2026

Authors Guidelines

  • Select the most appropriate conference track/theme when submitting.
  • Write in clear academic English; ensure accurate author names and affiliations.
  • Format figures, tables, and references per IEEE style.
  • At least one author must register and present the accepted paper.
  • Each accepted paper requires a separate registration.
  • For special session proposals, contact: s.vijayalakshmi@christuniversity.in.
    [Download Template & Call for Special Session Proposals]

For any queries: tqcebt.lavasa@christuniversity.in.

Presentation Guidelines

  • Conference dates: 24–26 March 2026, CHRIST University, Pune–Lavasa.
  • Suggested slot: 15 minutes presentation + 5 minutes Q&A (follow the session chair’s instructions).
  • Upload/Email your slides in PPT/PPTX or PDF as instructed; carry a backup on a USB drive.
  • Use large, readable fonts; prefer visuals, figures, and flowcharts over dense text.
  • Arrive at least 10 minutes early to your session for setup and checks.
  • Remote presentations (if permitted) will be coordinated separately with joining details.

Session chairs will manage time strictly. Outstanding talks may be considered for presentation awards.

The Microsoft CMT service was used for managing the peer-reviewing process for this conference. This service was provided for free by Microsoft and they bore all expenses, including costs for Azure cloud services as well as for software development and support.
